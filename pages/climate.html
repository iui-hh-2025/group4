AI for Climate Solutions

Exploring the positive potential of artificial intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly becoming a critical tool in the fight against climate change.
From improving weather forecasts to monitoring ecosystems and optimizing how we use natural resources, AI is helping scientists, governments, and communities make smarter, faster decisions. But AI is more than just a solution; it also raises important questions. Behind the scenes, AI systems require vast computational power, consuming large amounts of energy and water.
This page explores both sides: how AI is being used for good, and what it takes to power that progress.

Where AI Helps

Climate Prediction

AI improves forecasting of hurricanes, wildfires, floods, and other extreme events.

Environmental Monitoring

AI tools analyze satellite and sensor data to track ecosystem changes and pollution levels.

Sustainability Research

AI accelerates climate models and helps researchers understand complex environmental systems.

Recent advancements in artificial intelligence have revolutionized weather forecasting. Google DeepMind's GraphCast model exemplifies this progress by delivering 10-day weather forecasts with unprecedented accuracy and speed. Unlike traditional models that require extensive computational resources, GraphCast operates efficiently, providing rapid predictions that can aid in timely disaster preparedness.

Projects in Action

Google Earth Engine

Case Study: AI & Sea-Level Rise

AI is being used alongside NOAA and the National Water Prediction Service to monitor and model rising sea levels.

This helps planners prepare vulnerable communities for future flooding events.
